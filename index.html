About

I am a pre-doctoral researcher at the Indian Institute of Science (IISc) working on quantum computing systems — how quantum programs are compiled, planned, and executed reliably on real, noisy hardware — toward the goals of India's National Quantum Mission. Alongside this runs an applied second thread: anomaly detection for fraud and AML with NPCI, whose UPI network clears over 700M transactions a day.

I came to research through an Integrated M.Sc. in Mathematics at NIT Rourkela and two years building production data and machine-learning systems in industry. The combination of mathematical foundations and real systems at scale is what draws me to questions where quantum computing and applied machine learning meet real workloads.

Research interests

Quantum computers promise to expand what is computable — but today's machines are noisy, and coaxing a trustworthy answer out of them is a full-stack problem, from how a circuit is compiled to how its results are certified. My work spans that stack, toward quantum computation reliable enough to use — a goal at the core of India's National Quantum Mission.

Quantum compilation & systems

Full-stack co-design of the quantum stack: noise-adaptive compiler design and per-phase fidelity attribution (my HBR decomposition), together with cross-platform benchmarking of real NISQ hardware — treating compiler, runtime, and device as one system.

Reliable & feasible execution

From quantum search and optimization algorithms down to execution planning and shot-budget feasibility — sound resource estimates, and certifying when a precision target is actually reachable, so a result carries a guarantee rather than a hope.

Anomaly detection & hybrid ML

An applied thread on the same reliability question — graph neural networks for fraud and AML detection at production scale, and hybrid quantum–classical models on hard real-world data.
