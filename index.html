About

I am a pre-doctoral researcher at the Indian Institute of Science (IISc), working across two areas that share a hard problem: trusting the output of systems that are noisy and large. On the quantum side, I work toward the goals of India's National Quantum Mission, asking how reliable a near-term device's results really are. On the data side, I work with NPCI, whose UPI network clears over 700M transactions a day on anomaly detection for fraud and AML.

I came to research through an Integrated M.Sc. in Mathematics at NIT Rourkela and two years building production data and machine-learning systems in industry. The combination of mathematical foundations and real systems at scale is what draws me to questions where quantum computing and applied machine learning meet real workloads.

02

Research interests

Three directions, unified by a single concern — making computation reliable, from the quantum compiler down to production fraud systems.

Quantum compilation & systems

Noise-adaptive compilation and per-phase fidelity attribution across the compiler stack — gate decomposition, basis translation, routing. My HBR Decomposition framework localizes where fidelity is lost in real pipelines.

Trustworthy quantum computation

When can you trust the number a quantum device produces? I work on feasibility analysis and distribution-free guarantees that connect a precision target to a given device, circuit, and shot budget.

Anomaly detection at scale

Graph neural networks for fraud and AML detection on transaction graphs at production scale — reliability when the data is large, imbalanced, and adversarial.
